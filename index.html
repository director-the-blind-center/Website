How we help

Services built around independence and participation

The Center provides education, support, and referrals for people who are blind, visually impaired, older adults with vision loss, family members, caregivers, and community partners.

Assistive Technology

Support with devices, technology lab access, technical help, and tools that make everyday tasks more accessible.

Independent Living Skills

Classes and coaching for cooking, home tasks, finance, mobility-related confidence, and personal goals.

Programs and Classes

Health, wellness, creative studios, exercise, workshops, crafts, and community activities.

Eyewear and Resources

Help with emergency eyewear, paperwork, accommodations, referrals, and connection to social work support.

Work Readiness

Educational training, life skills, job training, and service coordination for people preparing for work or service roles.

Community Events

Fundraisers and seasonal gatherings that bring neighbors, donors, volunteers, vendors, and clients together.

Start here

Need support for yourself or someone you care about?

Contact the Center or visit in person. Staff can help with intake, discuss needs and goals, and connect people with programs, services, or referrals.

Read the Get Help steps
  1. Contact or visit. Call, email, or come to the Center at 221 N. Harvey St. in Washington, NC.
  2. Complete intake with assistance. Staff can help gather basic information and accessibility needs.
  3. Choose the next step. Explore programs, referrals, eyewear support, training, or accommodations.

Programs

Classes and activities for practical confidence

Cooking classes, health and wellness programming, exercise, DEEP diabetes education, yoga and meditation, healthier living, try-it workshops, weaving, printing, basket weaving, technology lab, open craft studios, and work-readiness support.
