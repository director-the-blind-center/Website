I'm supporting someone

Help without taking over.

Family members, caregivers, friends, and professionals can support someone with vision loss by listening first, encouraging independence, reducing barriers, and connecting to practical resources.

Ask for Caregiver Guidance

Support with respect

What does the person need help doing?

Listen

Understand the Change

Learn what has become harder, what still works well, and what professional eye-care guidance or urgent symptoms need attention.

Adapt

Make Home Safer

Improve lighting, reduce trip hazards, organize important items, label safely, and set up routines that the person can control.

Support

Encourage Independence

Offer help without removing choice. Ask before stepping in, practice new tools together, and connect to classes or peer support.

How the Center can help

Support works best when the person stays at the center of the plan.

The Blind Center can help supporters understand practical adaptations, explore caregiver resources, and connect families with learning, technology, eyewear, independent living, and community support.

  • Ask the person what they want help doing before changing routines or moving belongings.
  • Bring questions about safety, communication, technology, transportation, classes, or community resources.
  • Encourage the person to attend, call, or participate directly whenever possible.
