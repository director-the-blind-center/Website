Get Help and Resources

Start with a conversation about needs, goals, and next steps

The Blind Center connects blind and visually impaired people with intake support, assessments, services, training, accommodations, and referrals.

Contact the Center

How to begin

Staff can help complete intake and explain what information is needed.

  1. Contact or visit. Call, email, or come to 221 N. Harvey St., Washington, NC 27889.
  2. Complete intake. Share basic information, accessibility needs, and what kind of support you are seeking.
  3. Discuss needs and goals. Staff can help identify programs, training, eyewear support, referrals, or other resources.
  4. Connect with services. The Center may provide direct support or refer to partner services when needed.

Available resource areas

Eyewear Support

Emergency eyewear support may be available. Contact the Center for current eligibility and documentation needs.

Technical Support

Help with accessible technology, device questions, technology lab access, and practical digital skills.

Educational Training

Training to support independent living, self-advocacy, daily tasks, and program participation.

Life Skills

Support with home routines, paperwork, accommodations, and practical planning for everyday independence.

Job Training

Work-readiness support, goal planning, skill building, and referral pathways when available.

Referrals

Connections to social worker access, partner agencies, and services outside the Center's direct programs.

Accessible information and accommodations

Information should be available in plain language and accessible formats. If you need help reading a document, completing a form, or participating in a program, contact the Center before your visit when possible.
