My vision has changed

Start with clear information and a practical next step.

A change in vision can affect reading, driving, cooking, work, school, confidence, and daily routines. The Blind Center can help you sort out what to ask, what to try, and where to find support.

Talk Through My Next Step

What to do first

A simple path for understanding vision changes.

Step 1

Notice What Has Changed

Write down what is harder now: reading, glare, faces, steps, medication labels, screens, driving, or moving around safely.

Step 2

Protect Your Eye Health

For sudden vision loss, severe pain, flashes, new floaters, or a curtain-like shadow, seek urgent professional eye care.

Step 3

Learn Practical Options

Ask about lighting, contrast, magnification, large print, phone settings, transportation options, and community resources.

How the Center can help

You do not have to know the right program name.

The Blind Center can help you talk through what changed, identify practical supports to try, connect you with learning resources, and suggest next steps for glasses, technology, daily living, classes, or community resources.

  • Bring old glasses, a current prescription, eye doctor paperwork, or a list of questions if you have them.
  • Share the daily tasks that are hardest right now, even if you do not know the medical term for the condition.
  • Ask for large print, verbal help with forms, or another format that makes the visit easier.
