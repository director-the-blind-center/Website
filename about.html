About

Building futures through access, skills, and community

The Blind Center is a North Carolina 501(c)(3) nonprofit in Washington, NC, serving blind and visually impaired people with a focus on independence, dignity, and practical support.

Mission and service philosophy

The Center educates and empowers blind and visually impaired people by offering a welcoming place to learn, connect, and work toward personal goals.

Programs and services support assistive technology access, independent living skills, resources, referrals, creative activities, wellness, and community participation.

History

More than 40 years of community service

The Center was established by local community members and is administered by a Board of Directors. The original site also references client representation through the Beaufort County Association for the Blind and Visually Impaired.

Independence

Programs and resources are designed to support daily confidence, practical skills, and self-directed goals.

Inclusion

Services should be welcoming, respectful, and available through accessible communication paths.

Community

The Center brings together clients, caregivers, donors, volunteers, vendors, and partners.

Accessibility and inclusion statement

The Blind Center should make information available in accessible formats and provide reasonable accommodations for services, events, volunteering, and employment inquiries. Visitors can request help by phone, email, or in person.

Contact The Blind Center to ask about services, programs, accommodations, donations, volunteering, or community partnerships.
